About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology and space programs, he played a pivotal role in making India self-reliant in the fields of defense and space research.

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from humble beginnings. Despite financial struggles, he was a bright student who earned scholarships and later studied aerospace engineering. He worked at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he led the development of indigenous missile technology.

His Contributions to India

Dr. Kalam was instrumental in the success of the *Pokhran-II nuclear tests* in 1998, which made India a nuclear power. He also spearheaded the development of India's space technology, contributing significantly to programs such as the *SLV-III, which successfully deployed the **Rohini satellite* in Earth's orbit in 1980.

He also emphasized the importance of education and youth empowerment. Dr. Kalam's vision for India's future included the *Vision 2020 plan, aiming for the nation's growth in science and technology, infrastructure, and education. He was awarded the **Bharat Ratna*, India's highest civilian honor, in recognition of his contributions.

Legacy

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His simplicity, humility, and inspiring vision have left an indelible mark on millions of Indians. He remains a beloved figure and a true role model for students, scientists, and leaders worldwide.
